Options

Over here I share assessments of options whom we can vote for to run SL on our behalf.

0. Lehan Edirisinghe

This is my own self-assessment. It's biased.

Usefulness

I am a transparent leader with a good strategy for all the departments of my executive government and the two arms of government: parliament and courts, to serve our people.

We show good, entertaining journal keeping on how I am thinking.

There is a good ambitious SLWF strategy, together with a creative, inclusive SLFT program. This is all very different from the other politicians.

There is no emphasis or requiring of persons to depend on me or ask me for things which is a source of relief.

We can transform SL, and trasfigure our society. We leverage modern tools, skills and applied strategies to do so.

Drawbacks

Sri Lanka's a divided, factional nation. There is a lot of lobbying demand for bad policies or businesses. Particularly by vocal groups who don't know how a country should be governed. The corrupt culture of SL has been destructive to many people.

I worry there will be "resistance" by the established business people, who will get support from the overwhelming majority of people here, against me.

They will all say my ideas of doing "unethical, bad karma" generating businesses which Siddhartha Gautama's banned, is wrong, or immoral: I will be rejected from society, lose the election and someone else will win the vote.

They will then continue on the destructive path to failure SL is on, as they will be representing the people's will to do so.

Self-conclusions

A lot of people have given up on the people of Sri Lanka, by ruling that "hey this is Sri Lanka". They mean that the people are the problem, are a hopeless lost case, and it's better to play along, leave or go do something else.

As failure and resulting frustration has become institutionalized as an integral part of our SL system, fabric and identity. We're not just an underdog, it's a pale horse in a horse race, where we are unlikely at all to win.

However, I have confidence that the people here, will hear my message, will think for themselves, and become the solutions they seek themselves by voting for me. It represents modernity. With enough campaigning, we can win our election.

Uncertainty

It's like someone from a developed country has shown up. Has identified problems, and has come up with a proposal to be the dictator, to apply solutions.

These solutions are all systems and strategies that work in developed countries. But those are predominatly either Christian or secular states. Plus white or Asian Yellow. They are accepting of those systems that work.

The best outcome is we're accepted, the wrong outcome is we're rejected, and then the people fail, and some of them escape to a developed country which does what I tried to do here. So they are able to thrive, be free and happy.

1. Ranil Wickramsinghe

After several terms as PM of a failed state. While inflation is still worse than it was with Gota. He has cut imports, stopped protests, etc. He is now asking to be president. But there is no clear vision of what this entails.

Value

He is a mature, stable and professional leader. He has decades of insight, often as a leader, to guide his moves. He is very likely to have written a better history from 2005 for us, had he won the election against Mahinda Rajapaksa. He stepped up, bravely to face the challenge our country faced, scorned and covered with scars, with 1 out of 225 MP seats, and he was able to secure our people's sense of life. He has patience, strong values and is respected by everyone here for he respects them. He has even helped me see Lee Hsien Loong once, which is nice of him. He will position us well, better than alternatives, but we would have to do everything to make the most of it.

Trouble

In addition to being quite unpatriotic. He has failed to understand SL's problems are structural. These problems we have are in-built fail-sure mechanisms. These problems require a bold, courageous and ambitious leader who will face them head on. Sometimes getting along with bad people, when things are going wrong, is not a strength, but a weakness. Theoretical grasp of free market policies aren't enough. You need to be able to show pragmatic decision making, very directly. The free market isn't going to bode well for us, we can't survive as a small country, we will need to be clever. You will need to show real leadership, by not playing it safe.

Mismatch

A real leader has to first make sure the industries here are booming, so there is a domestic economy. This means standing up to the lobby of the monks preaching Right Livelihood. It means bringing real criminals who steal to justice, not Jerome Fernando for preaching his faith in Christ to his audience. Although Ranil saw public finances are "cooked", he has done nothing, he's lied about people being fed when 20% starve in a man-made disaster. If he came to a developed country, asking to be PM or President there, they'd see in his CV he was PM mutliple times in a disaster nation. Be a hero the developed world will come to, and then we're already there ourselves.

2. Anura Kumara Dissanayake

He has got a lot of attention finding fault. He won a lot of respect for having done so. However, he hasn't proposed any solutions. He isn't even revealing clearly what his communist utopia will look like for 99 of us.

The Good

While many will say communism doesn't work. It has evolved, with state capitalist China, and some say Singapore too. They pay for socialist objectives with funds acquired from free market principles such as efficiency in Singapore. China in a short time was able to conquer the whole world, while India is a derailed train wreck in perpetuity as a democracy. The truth has dawned on us all that the man is one of the most honest, humble and serious. He has got not just vision in his mind, but love like Che in his heart, and a rebel spirit like we saw when any nation overthrew their colonial masters or their puppet appointees.

The Bad

While I might have strong plans. Some of them are far-fetched, likely to result in aversion from destractors. I still share them. Anura doesn't. This is something that does make us feel worried, as we would rather know where we are headed. The 9/11 hijackers may very well have lied on their way to Islamic Jihad paradise. So it doesn't fully matter. However, his momentum will soon run out, with his popularity free-falling to 25%. As people are worried he wouldn't honor debts to BlackRock as he claims, despite SL entering those agreements with lenders.

The Ugly

While I won't call him a "comrade" as I dislike communism. I just don't believe in centrally planned government. I believe in rights, success and freedom of the individual. I will be proud to call him my comrade in arms, as I see a patriot. I would fight by his side. But not if he wants to rob our people's right to own stuff, to pay the state. It's then a sibling rivalry. As he's not educated properly, they often say they don't understand or disagree, when a sophisticated issue, for lack of cultural exposure. It's wrong when untrained doctors who can't treat patients are deciding.

The Pretty Penny

There is a big risk, that the same slip into the left, which we saw from UNP Ranasinghe Premadasa to PA Chandrika Kumaratunga, then more to left with Mahinda Rajapaksa, Sirisena and Gotabaya, will culminate in our ruination. As they expect the people to trust the gov't more, to pay more in taxes, to surrender rights, to not have property rights or rights to engage in business freely or invest. Commies expect all 99 out of 100 to bow down before Anura to be allowed to do business, for bigger "needs" handouts, as he turns into Joseph Stalin, a communist dictator.

He played by the rules our people gave him, instead of fighting them, he was able to thrive. Put simply: there are no real courts, no sense of confidence internally or externally, and too many negative news reports.

Honestly: I like Chamath so much, I'd vote for him. He is a SL citizen. But the problem is he hasn't got as good a vision for what he would like Sri Lanka to be.

This lack of a clear vision is lacking. I propose total change which I believe in. I can invite him as my first finance minister (which is the big one).

Pros

He's someone who saw FB wasn't healthy, pointed it out. He has invested SPACs with great care for ethics to build returns. There will always be naysayers and critics. So people will condemn him on his Wikipedia. As public figures, anyone who can think of a cruel thing to write will do so.

If I could be any Sinhalese, I would choose to be him. He is a very cool person. Whom I admire. He's got great intuition, charm and character. He's like Upali Wijewardene.

So, as a Sinhalese race, who represent Sri Lanka for the most part and as human beings who care for each other: we all are deeply appreciative of a Trump presidency. He is friends with several cool people in the USA.

He hasn't been here to see what's going on. There are many systematic breakdowns. Which are so deep, structural and iceberg-like, not merely skeletons buried, that it's not as easy as it seems to fix this place.

This is perhaps a good thing as he has a will to improve things here--after escaping hell whereas everyone else has already completely given up.
